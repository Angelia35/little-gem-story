Auralis Jewelry Content and Disclosure Standards

How Auralis writes product guidance, checks current order terms, labels uncertain material information, handles symbolic Five Elements content and updates pages when business rules change.

Author: Auralis Jewelry · Reviewer: Auralis Jewelry — order and disclosure check · Updated: July 24, 2026

Our standard

Clearer than a marketing promise

A page should tell a buyer what is confirmed, what is only a visual reference and what still requires an order-specific quote or material check.

View Verified Specifications

Evidence Labels

Four types of information appear on Auralis pages

Confirmed Auralis policy

Current business rules

Examples include full-custom starting guidance, included revision count, deposit percentage and typical production period. These are reviewed when Auralis changes its order process.

Order-specific

Details that require a quote

Exact stone, bead diameter, treatment status, accessories, shipping, stock, final price and timing must be confirmed for the proposed design.

Design reference

Comparison rather than a guarantee

Color moods, bead-size impressions and style categories help customers communicate a direction but do not promise current inventory.

Draft / unpublished evidence

Not presented as a real case

Case-study templates remain noindex until real order data, supporting evidence and appropriate anonymization are available.

Review Process

How a page is prepared and maintained

1

Check current Auralis terms

Pricing language, deposit, revisions, production timing and payment sequence are checked against the current order process before publication.

2

Separate facts from symbolic meaning

Five Elements, color symbolism and cultural inspiration are described as personal reflection or design direction, not guaranteed medical, financial, spiritual or future outcomes.

3

Limit material claims to available evidence

Auralis does not use blanket claims such as every stone being untreated, certified or laboratory verified unless the specific item has supporting evidence.

4

Add sources where outside context is needed

GIA and FTC sources may be cited for general treatment, terminology and disclosure context. Citations do not mean those organizations endorse Auralis products.

5

Show author, review scope and update date

Core decision pages include visible responsibility information and a last-updated date.

6

Correct pages when evidence changes

Customers may report a wording or factual concern to hello@auralisgems.com. Material corrections should be made without hiding the scope of uncertainty.

Case Evidence

No invented customers, testimonials or order outcomes

What a publishable anonymous case needs

  • A real inquiry or order record
  • Personal details removed
  • Budget shown as a band where needed
  • Actual design inputs and recommendation route
  • Real revision count and production timeline
  • Evidence files retained privately

What Auralis will not present as evidence

  • A fictional testimonial
  • An illustrative scenario labelled as a completed order
  • A generated customer photo presented as UGC
  • A laboratory or certification claim without a real report
  • An outcome claim that cannot be supported
