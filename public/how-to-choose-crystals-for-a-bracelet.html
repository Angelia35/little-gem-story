Quick answer

The easiest way to choose crystals for a bracelet is to decide five things first: budget, color mood, bead size, wrist size and wearing purpose. After that, ask about the exact material name, treatment status, natural variation and price before confirming the order.

FAQ

Questions about choosing crystals

How do I choose crystals for a bracelet if I do not know stone names?

Start with your budget, preferred color mood, wrist size, wearing style and gift purpose. Auralis can then recommend suitable available or custom material directions before quote confirmation.

Does a higher price always mean a better bracelet?

No. Price can change with material type, bead size, visual grade, accessories, treatment status, craftsmanship and design complexity. The best option is the one that fits the customer's budget, style and use.

What bead size should I choose?

Smaller beads usually look more subtle, while larger beads create a stronger statement. Final bead size should be confirmed together with wrist size, material availability and the desired design.

Can Auralis recommend ready-to-ship and custom options together?

Yes. Customers can share a budget first. Auralis may recommend ready-to-ship, simple personalization or fully custom-made directions depending on current availability and the request.

Are the stone meanings guaranteed effects?

No. Color, cultural meaning and symbolic intention can inspire a design, but they do not guarantee medical, financial, spiritual or future outcomes.
